## Understanding Sports Betting Pick Types

  

Before diving into whether Lev's Locks picks are worth following, you should understand the different types of bets their analysts recommend. This context helps you evaluate their picks more intelligently.

  

### Moneyline Bets

  

A moneyline bet is the simplest form of sports wagering—you pick a team to win outright, no point spread involved. The favored team pays lower odds while underdogs pay higher odds for the same stake.

  

For example, if a football team is listed at -150, you'd need to bet $150 to win $100. If the underdog is +130, a $100 bet wins you $130.

  

### Point Spread Bets

  

Point spreads level the playing field between mismatched teams. The favorite "gives" points while the underdog "takes" points.

  

If the Miami Heat are favored by 3.5 points over Oklahoma City, Miami needs to win by 4 or more points for a spread bet on them to cash. OKC can win outright or lose by 3 or fewer and still win the bet.

  

This is the most common type of bet in American sports betting, and you'll likely see plenty of spread picks in Lev's Locks recommendations.

  

### Over/Under (Total) Bets

  

These wagers focus on combined scoring from both teams, not which team wins. A bookmaker sets a total like 215 points for an NBA game.

  

An "over" bettor wants the final combined score above 215. An "under" bettor wants it below 215. If the final total matches exactly, most books refund your stake (a "push").

  

### Parlay Bets

  

Parlays combine multiple bets into one wager. Your winnings from the first bet roll over into the second, then the third, and so on. All picks must win for the parlay to pay out.

  

The appeal is massive payouts—parlays reward successful bettors with exponentially higher returns than single wagers. However, parlays account for over 70% of sportsbook revenue in the U.S. because they're difficult to hit consistently.

  

If you see Lev's Locks offering parlay picks, understand you're taking on higher risk for higher potential reward.

  

### Teaser Bets

  

A teaser is a modified parlay that gives you an advantage by moving the point spread in your favor, but with lower payouts than a regular parlay.

  

For example, instead of betting two NBA games at normal spreads, you might get +6.5 extra points on each side. This makes winning more likely, but you collect less profit if successful.

  

### Proposition Bets

  

Props are wagers on specific outcomes unrelated to the final score. Examples include predicting how many yards a running back will gain, how many goals a player scores, or which team scores first.

  

These are more specialized bets requiring deeper individual player and matchup analysis.

  

### Futures Bets

  

Futures have long-term horizons measured in weeks or months. A typical future might be betting whether a specific NFL team wins the Super Bowl for the upcoming season.

  

These bets stay open until the event concludes, and sportsbooks typically prefer them because they hold customer money longer.

  

### Live Betting

  

Live betting, or in-play betting, lets you place new wagers while games are actively happening. Markets update in real-time based on game action.

  

This is popular among sharp bettors who can quickly react to changing circumstances, but it also poses higher risk due to rapid decision-making without time for reflection.

## Sports Betting Legality and Important Disclaimers

  

### Current Legal Status in the United States

  

Prior to 2018, sports betting was effectively illegal nationwide under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) from 1992. Only Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon—which had pre-existing sports betting frameworks—were exempted.

  

The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, opening the door for state-by-state legalization.

  

As of January 2024, the landscape includes:

-   37 states with operational legal sports betting
-   Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico with legal sports betting
-   30 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico with legal online sports betting
-   8 states restricting betting to physical sportsbooks only
-   North Carolina with legalized betting launching later in 2024

  

Since PASPA's strike down, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports in the United States alone.

  

### Key Point: Check Your Jurisdiction

  

Sports betting legality varies significantly by location. Some states prohibit it entirely, some restrict it to physical locations, and some allow full online betting.

  

**Your responsibility:** Verify that online sports betting is legal in your specific state, city, and country before placing any bets.

## Related Betting Concepts to Understand

  

While using Lev's Locks picks, you'll benefit from understanding these additional betting concepts:

  

### Bankroll Management

  

Your betting bankroll is separate money you've designated specifically for wagering. Sound bankroll management means risking only 1-2% per pick, never betting money you need for bills or emergencies.

  

### Expected Value (EV)

  

Expected value in sports betting represents the average profit or loss you'd expect per bet over many repetitions. Positive EV picks win money long-term despite short-term variance.

  

### Variance and Swings

  

Even great picks experience winning and losing streaks due to randomness. Understanding variance prevents panic during downswings and overconfidence during upswings.

  

### Odds Shopping

  

Different sportsbooks offer slightly different odds on the same bets. Comparing odds across multiple books and using the best available line significantly impacts long-term profitability.
